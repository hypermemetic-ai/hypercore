# queue

## the operator can speak from anywhere

> The operator can speak from anywhere in the interface: their words land on disk verbatim, the place they spoke from travels with them, and nothing more is asked of them. [machine]

- section: foundations
- context: the first slice has one text entry — explain words on a card the machine drafted. the operator can answer but cannot begin. this generalizes explain-words into one standing move: speak, from any view, words landing verbatim with their context attached.
- approve: the next interface slice grows a speak key in every view; the operator's words become first-class records on disk, the seed every other motion grows from.
- cut: the operator keeps engaging the machine only through conversation or through explain on machine-drafted cards; the interface stays answer-only, and the third statement (reading the words is machine work) has nothing to read.

## reading the operator's words is machine work

> Reading the operator's words is machine work. Whether they are new intent, a new ask, an answer, or a redirect is the machine's call to make and answer for; what it made of them returns through the queue. [machine]

- section: foundations
- context: the operator's attention is the scarcest resource and classification is bookkeeping. the operator speaks a sentence; the machine decides what kind of thing the sentence is and files it where it belongs.
- approve: the machine answers for misreadings the way it answers for any machine-owned call; what it made of the words always comes back visible, so a misreading is catchable in the queue, not buried.
- cut: the operator must label their own words at the moment of speaking — ask or intent or redirect, a form instead of a sentence — which puts graph bookkeeping in the operator's hands at every utterance.

## the queue is a view, not a place

> The queue is a view, not a place: a decision lives where it arises — on a statement, on a node, on evidence — and the queue is read live from all of it. Nothing is moved into the queue or out of it. [machine]

- section: foundations
- context: with ratification as the only source, document order was order enough. sources are about to multiply — graphs that cannot fold, evidence against owned statements, answers to the operator's words. a queue that is its own structure must be groomed by someone; a view cannot lag and cannot be groomed.
- approve: the queue is always read live from wherever decisions arose; no add, remove, or move operation on the queue itself may ever be built.
- cut: the queue becomes a real list someone maintains, and the only candidates are the machine (more machinery to trust) or the operator (graph maintenance — the ceremony alarm already in foundations).

## the order of the queue is a machine claim that answers for itself

> The order of the queue is the machine's claim about what the operator's attention is worth next, and it answers for that claim: every decision wears the cost of its own delay — what it blocks, what compounds while it waits — and the operator reorders with a word, never by hand. [machine]

- section: foundations
- context: priority decisions are coming and the question is who pays for priority. a stored priority field must be tended; a worn one is derived from what each decision blocks and what compounds while it waits, costliest first.
- approve: the machine owns the order and shows its grounds on each card; a patterned operator override is evidence against those grounds and surfaces through the existing coherence statement, not silently absorbed.
- cut: order stays document order until that visibly breaks, and priority then arrives the default way — a stored field someone must groom.
