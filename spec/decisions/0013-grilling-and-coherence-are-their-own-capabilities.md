# ADR 0013 ŌĆö grilling and coherence are their own capabilities

Status: machine-owned, ratified in shape by the operator (role-assembly step 4b, 2026-06-21):
the operator chose to **carve a capability** rather than render a skill from a requirement-subset,
and to skill **both** of the architect's remaining methodologies. The requirement allocation below
(which requirements move, and that coherence takes the depth-gate judgment with the coherence
check) is the machine-side detail of that decision. [machine]

## Context

Item 2 (ADR 0009) carries the architect's specialization in **skills**, each rendered from a clean
standalone capability slice (`spec/<cap>/spec.md` ŌåÆ `skills/<cap>/SKILL.md`) and regenerated by the
fold (`methodology.py`, `channels.py`). Slice 12 skilled the two architect methodologies that were
already their own capabilities ŌĆö `design-it-twice` (ADR 0007) and `architecture-review` (ADR 0005).
ADR 0009 ┬¦1 names two more: the **grilling** pass (pre-work intent extraction) and the **coherence**
judgment (integrating a worker's hand-off at the archive gate). Both lived as requirements *inside*
`conversation`, so they had no slice to render ŌĆö `methodology.materialize` could not reach them.

The seam had to be cut one way, and step 4b put the cut to the operator (role-assembly `intent.md`):

- **carve a capability** ŌĆö move the requirements into their own `spec/<cap>/spec.md`, so they render
  through the existing methodology seam with no new code; or
- **a requirement-subset render** ŌĆö keep `conversation` whole and teach `methodology` to render a
  skill from a *named subset* of a capability's requirements.

The subset render avoids restructuring but reintroduces what the channels mechanism exists to kill:
naming the subset needs either an in-spec grouping (a restructuring anyway) or an external list ŌĆö a
second copy of the requirement set that drifts the moment a requirement is renamed. The whole-slice
render carries no such list, because the subset *is* the whole slice. ADR 0001 set the decomposition
discipline; ADR 0003 (worker), ADR 0004 (folding-conditions), and ADR 0005 (architecture-review) each
cut a new top-level capability when it owned a surface none of the others did, against ADR 0002's
counter-precedent of folding behavior into an existing capability when it owns no surface of its own.

## Decision

**Grilling and coherence are each their own capability, carved from `conversation`.** Each owns a
distinct methodological surface the others do not:

- **`grilling`** ŌĆö the pre-work intent-extraction methodology (the floor, the one-at-a-time interview
  with a lean and a flip, the standing guard, the contract + spec-delta products). Its code is already
  its own module (`grill.py`), so capability and module now align. Requirements moved out of
  `conversation`: *a filed ask is grilled before it becomes work*; *grilling asks one question at a
  time, each carrying a lean*; *a grilling pass yields the contract and the spec delta*.

- **`coherence`** ŌĆö the architect's judgment at the **archive gate**: the coherence check against the
  contract at the operator's altitude (not a code review) and the **depth** decision, both raising a
  decision rather than a silent pass or veto, both fired in the one `integrate` act. Taking the depth
  gate with the coherence check makes this one coherent surface ŌĆö the architect's verdict over a
  worker's product ŌĆö rather than splitting one gate's judgment across two capabilities. Requirements
  moved out of `conversation`: *the architect integrates the worker's hand-off*; *the architect judges
  depth at the archive gate*.

`conversation` keeps the **thread** and the **single operator-facing voice** ŌĆö the throwaway session,
the three-consequences turn, the off-loop summons, the raw-output-never-reaches-the-operator invariant,
and the design-it-twice selection. The architect remains the single actor that *runs* grilling and
coherence; the capabilities are the methodologies it runs, not separate voices.

Both are registered in `methodology.METHODOLOGIES` and render through the same seam as the other two
architect skills, single-sourced from their slices and materialized by the fold.

## Grounds

The cut falls where the system already cuts (ADR 0005's test): each carved unit owns a surface none of
the others do ŌĆö `grilling` the pre-work extraction, `coherence` the archive-gate judgment ŌĆö distinct
from the thread and voice `conversation` keeps. The decisive grounds is the single-source discipline
ADR 0009 is built on: the whole-slice render has **no drift surface**, where the subset render would
add a second copy of the requirement set to keep in sync ŌĆö the exact thing the materialize-on-fold
mechanism exists to make impossible. Carving costs a spec restructuring once; the subset render would
cost a standing drift risk forever. No behavior changes ŌĆö the code (`grill.py`, `conversation.integrate`)
is untouched; only the requirements' home moves, so the worker (which holds the whole spec regardless)
and every acceptance check see the same system.

## Consequences

The operator view is eleven units wide: `interface`, `graph`, `queue`, `conversation`, `self-model`,
`worker`, `folding-conditions`, `architecture-review`, `design-it-twice`, `grilling`, `coherence`.
`conversation` loses five requirements and gains nothing; `grilling` and `coherence` are new slices
holding them verbatim. `methodology.METHODOLOGIES` gains two entries, and the fold now materializes
`skills/grilling/SKILL.md` and `skills/coherence/SKILL.md` beside the others ŌĆö role-assembly step 4b,
the last of ADR 0009 ┬¦1's four architect methodologies. A future change that re-cuts these boundaries
(folding either back into `conversation`, or carrying coherence's depth-gate judgment elsewhere) carries
an ADR superseding this one.
