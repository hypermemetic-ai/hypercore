# sweep

the sweep checks coherence between a change and the rest of the intent.
the sweep runs in two passes: the map follows a concept to everywhere it appears and narrows the field; the read judges the relation among those.
the map is cheap; the read is the costly, valuable pass.
the read is semantic and therefore graded, not certain: it rates a clash, it does not prove one.
the sweep flags; the proof settles; no decision rests on the sweep's probability.
the sweep runs at frame and again at check, both over the whole corpus and the changes in flight.
the map spans everything written — the intent and machine-statements/ alike — and relations are read across all of it.
the sweep reads a child node's intent against its parent's contract, the same as it reads any related statements, so coherence across the node boundary is the sweep's to hold, not check.sh's.
the corpus is open-world: absence is never a verdict; the only invariant is that whatever is leaned on is grounded somewhere.
a machine statement that something comes to lean on is copied up into that segment's `## machine`, and the original stays in machine-statements/.
where two names are surely one concept and merging loses no clarity, merge them by taking a name out; where it is unsure or the distinction is real, surface it, do not guess.
