# sweep

The sweep reads semantic coherence between written claims.

The sweep maps where a concept appears.

The sweep reads those appearances together.

The sweep flags clashes, naming drift, hidden assumptions, missing ground, and possible
false distinctions.

The sweep is semantic and therefore fallible.

The sweep does not prove truth.

A contradiction between parent and child matters only where the parent's statement
actually reaches the child.

Absence is not a verdict.

The corpus is open-world: only what the system leans on must be grounded somewhere.

Where two names are surely one concept and merging loses no clarity, merge them by taking
one name out.

Where a distinction is real or uncertain, surface it rather than guessing it away.
