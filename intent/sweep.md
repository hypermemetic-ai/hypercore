# sweep

the sweep checks coherence between work and the rest of the intent.
the sweep runs in two passes: the map follows a concept to everywhere it appears and
narrows the field; the read judges the relation among those statements.
the map is cheap; the read is the costly, valuable pass.
the read is semantic and therefore graded, not certain: it rates a clash, it does not prove
one.
the sweep flags; the proof settles; no decision rests on the sweep's probability.
the sweep runs at frame and again at check, over the whole corpus and the work in flight
across the node tree, including related work named by a coordinating frame.
the sweep reads a child work node against its parent intent, its proposed parent
amendments, sibling work, machine statements, and every current node whose concepts it
touches.
the sweep reads a work frame's common-ground record as part of the corpus relation, not
only as proposed parent amendments.
the sweep distinguishes a child statement that must already satisfy the parent from a
proposed parent amendment that is not parent truth until adoption.
a contradiction between a child and the parent blocks unless the frame explicitly presents
it as a proposed amendment for adoption.
when work names related work, the sweep reads those deltas together wherever their nodes
are so related work cannot silently contradict the coordinating scope or a child node's
parent contract.
the map spans everything written -- the intent and `machine-statements/` alike -- and
relations are read across all of it.
the sweep reads a child node's intent against the parent statements whose reach includes
that child, so coherence across the node boundary is the sweep's to hold, not
`check.sh`'s.
the corpus is open-world: absence is never a verdict; the only invariant is that whatever
is leaned on is grounded somewhere.
a machine statement that something comes to lean on is copied up into that segment's
`## machine`, and the original stays in `machine-statements/`.
where two names are surely one concept and merging loses no clarity, merge them by taking a
name out; where it is unsure or the distinction is real, surface it, do not guess.
