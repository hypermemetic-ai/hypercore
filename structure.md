# structure

Intent starts work; material is what the work makes. Intent arrives whole from the operator — rarely — or comes out of back-and-forth between operator and machine. It constrains the problem space; each decision along the way narrows it further. Finished material is validated against the intent and the decisions that narrowed it. All of this serves operator knowledge: the operator's internal model of the system matches what gets built and what ends up in the real world. [machine]

The intent lives at the root, as the markdown files there. Everything else in the repository is material. [machine]

The engine is material. It is the implementation that stores and serves the graph. [machine]

Nodes are operations. Intent statements are not nodes: they live in their own store with their own index. An operation points to the statements it produces and is bound by the statements it acts under; both are references the operation carries, not edges to statement nodes. [machine]
