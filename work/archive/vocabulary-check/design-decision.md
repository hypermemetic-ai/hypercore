# design-it-twice: the watched-evidence trace seam [machine]

A load-bearing interface for 'the watched-evidence trace' was designed twice — candidates
consumer-driven, gate-inferred, node-marker, each fenced, each built to a different brief — and
compared on depth, locality, and seam placement. The seam is load-bearing because two consumers ride
it: the vocabulary check now and the model-driven depth scan next. Three decisions are entangled in it
— where the verdict trace lives and its format, how the gate learns a fold carries a watched standard
(so it knows to expect a trace), and how `reachability` composes the new clause.

design-decision: the watched-evidence trace → consumer-driven presence seam (hybridized with the gate-inferred trail's committed-artifact format) — the watched standard, which alone knows it ran, asks the gate to attest its verdict trace's presence by mechanism name; the gate stays a pure, capability-agnostic presence checker over a committed per-mechanism artifact, never inferring which standards apply and so never demanding a trail on a fold that carries no watched standard.

## Grounds

- **consumer-driven (chosen)**: `provenance.watched_trace(node, mechanism, root)` is a standalone seam returning the flat `NO_TRAIL` when the named mechanism's committed verdict trace is absent, None when present; `provenance.commit_verdict(node, mechanism, verdict, root)` is its writer. The watched standard's own fold guard (the vocabulary check, in `conditions`) calls it when its standard is in play. Deepest on locality — the decision to *require* a trace lives with the standard that knows it ran, so the gate hides "what a committed watched verdict is" behind a two-argument presence call and learns no capability's content (the mechanism is a label that forms the trace path, never inspected). The seam falls exactly between "a watched run happened" (the consumer's knowledge) and "its verdict durably survives" (the gate's check). `reachability` is left unchanged — a fold carrying no watched standard demands nothing — and the new clause composes by the consumer invoking it, so it serves the depth scan unchanged by passing a different mechanism name.
- **gate-inferred**: fold the clause into `reachability` unconditionally, the gate reading the delta's touched capabilities against a registry of watched-trace standards and demanding a committed trace for each. Rejected on locality and on the capability-agnostic bar: the gate would hold a registry coupling it to named capabilities — the one thing the contract forbids ("never any one capability's content") — and the trigger is wrong, since the vocabulary check runs on *every* fold over the whole corpus, not keyed to a delta touching `communication`; unconditional composition also demands a trace on every fold, refusing every legitimate fold that carries no watched standard. Its one strength — a durable committed artifact symmetric with the accepted-length record's writer/reader pair — is **hybridized into the pick**: the trace is a committed `<mechanism>.verdict.md` on the node, attested by `git` presence exactly as `_accepted_length` attests the committed ledger.
- **node-marker**: the node carries a self-asserted marker declaring a watched run was due, and the gate refuses when the marker stands without a matching verdict. Rejected outright — a node-side "a watched run was expected" flag is the forgeable self-asserting record this gate already forbids (the `ran:true` antipattern, `the provenance trail … never a self-asserted flag`): the thing that proves the expectation is the thing whose absence is the lie, so it is circular and leaves no real trail.
