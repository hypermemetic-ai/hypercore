# statements

A segment's intent document is that segment's current intended reality.

A statement is declarative and strong enough to be wrong.

Statements are written plainly.

One name means one concept.

Behavior and dependency are both written as statements when they matter.

Every statement should be ownable.

Every statement should be falsifiable against material, evidence, or later judgment.

A statement that is neither ownable nor falsifiable is not ready for intent.

Ownership is the right to change a statement, not permission to break coherence.

The operator and the machine are both bound by coherence.

When the operator leaves a choice open and the machine settles it, that settlement is visible as machine-owned intent until the operator takes it on.
